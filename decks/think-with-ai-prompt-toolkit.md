# Q1: What Is This For, and Who Will See It?

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-10 mt-6 items-start">
  <div>
    <div class="text-[14px] text-hg-dark mb-5">Be specific. Not "a blog post" but "a blog post for VP-level buyers who already know our category and are evaluating vendors." The more precisely you define the audience and context, the more precisely Claude can calibrate tone, depth, and language.</div>
    <div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
      <div class="flex items-start gap-3">
        <span class="flex-shrink-0 w-6 h-6 rounded-full bg-hg-red text-white flex items-center justify-center text-[11px] font-bold mt-0.5">✗</span>

      </div>
      <div class="flex items-start gap-3">

# Q3: What Would Make You Reject This Output?

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-10 mt-6 items-start">
  <div>
    <div class="text-[14px] text-hg-dark mb-3">Name your dealbreakers. This is the most important question and the one people skip most often. It tells Claude what failure looks like and prevents it from delivering something technically competent but fundamentally wrong.</div>
    <div class="mt-4 border-l-4 border-hg-red bg-red-50 pl-4 py-3 rounded-r-md">
      <div class="font-bold text-hg-red text-[13px] mb-1">Why people skip this</div>
      <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark">It requires you to think about failure before you've started. That feels pessimistic. But it's the fastest path to work that actually lands.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-hg-light rounded-xl p-6 border-l-4 border-hg-red">
    <div class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[13px] uppercase tracking-wide mb-3">Example</div>
    <div class="text-[14px] text-hg-dark italic">"I'd reject it if it sounds like every other B2B vendor, leads with features instead of the problem, or uses phrases like 'actionable insights,' 'best-in-class,' or 'accelerate your pipeline.'"</div>

# 1. Counter Views

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">See the other side before you commit to yours.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What are the strongest arguments against this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The fastest way to stress-test your own thinking. If Claude can construct a strong counter-argument, so can the person you're pitching.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would a skeptic say about this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Puts you in the shoes of your toughest critic before you're in the room with them. Surfaces the objections you've been unconsciously avoiding.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Steel-man the opposing view."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">A step beyond devil's advocate. The goal is the strongest possible version of the argument against you, not just surface-level critique.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What does the person who disagrees with me most believe, and why?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Forces you to take the opposing view seriously. You can't counter an argument you haven't actually understood.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Summarize the strongest point on each side in one sentence each."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Good for getting unstuck when you're going in circles. Boils a complex debate down to its real core tension.</span></div>
  </div>
</div>

# 2. Challenge the Idea

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Poke holes before someone else does.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would need to be true for this to be wrong?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">One of the most powerful prompts in the list. Forces you to name your assumptions explicitly instead of letting them hide inside your logic.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"If this fails, what's the most likely reason?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Pre-mortem thinking. Much easier to spot failure modes before you're emotionally invested in the outcome.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Where are the gaps or holes in this thinking?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">A direct invitation to find what's broken. Works best after you've already drafted something and need fresh eyes on it.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What am I assuming that might be wrong?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Most bad decisions aren't caused by bad logic; they're caused by unchallenged assumptions. This makes those assumptions visible.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the version of this argument that would make me most uncomfortable?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Pushes past polite critique into the thing you don't want to hear. Uncomfortable = usually important.</span></div>
  </div>
</div>

# 3. What's Missing

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Surface the blind spots.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the most important thing I haven't considered?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The open-ended version of gap analysis. Useful when you don't know what you don't know and need a broader scan.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What question should I be asking that I'm not asking?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Often the framing of a problem is the problem. This challenges whether you're even asking the right question in the first place.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What context would someone need before this makes sense?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Surfaces the invisible prerequisites. Most communication failures happen because the sender assumed shared context that doesn't exist.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would an expert in this area say I'm overlooking?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Simulates the feedback you'd get from someone with deep domain knowledge. Useful before sharing work with someone who actually has that expertise.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What are the unstated assumptions baked into this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Focused on the output itself rather than the reasoning. What beliefs had to be true for this to make sense?</span></div>

# 4. Reframe It

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Go sideways, not just deeper.</div>

  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Describe this problem from the customer's perspective instead of ours."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The internal view and the customer view are almost never the same. This forces the shift before the customer has to make it for you.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Explain this as if it's a product launch. What's the headline?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The product launch frame forces clarity on the "so what." Useful for anything that needs to generate excitement or urgency.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"How would you frame this to convince a skeptic, not a believer?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Most content is written for people who already agree. This prompt rewrites it for the person who doesn't.</span></div>

  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Rewrite this as a story instead of an argument."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Arguments get debated; stories get remembered. When logic isn't landing, narrative often will.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the version of this that would excite someone who has never heard of it?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Strips away insider language and forces genuine clarity. If you can't make it interesting to a newcomer, the framing needs work.</span></div>

# 5. Expand Your Thinking

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Push the boundaries of what's possible.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What are the second and third-order consequences of this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Most people stop at the first-order effect. This forces you to think about what happens next, and what happens after that.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the version of this that's 10x more ambitious?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Breaks you out of incremental thinking. Even if you don't pursue the 10x version, it often surfaces possibilities you'd have otherwise ignored.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Give me three completely different ways to approach this problem."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Forces optionality before you commit. The first approach is rarely the best one; it's just the most obvious.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"If you had no constraints, what would you do?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Separates the ideal from the feasible. Useful for understanding what you actually want before you start negotiating with reality.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What does this look like in 3 years if it works exactly as intended?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Builds the vision backward. Useful when you're too close to the tactical details to see where you're actually headed.</span></div>
  </div>

# 6. How to Make It Better

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Improve before you ship.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would make this significantly stronger?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The direct improvement prompt. Sometimes the most useful thing is simply asking what's holding the work back from being excellent.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the weakest part of this, and how would you fix it?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Pinpoints the specific thing that, if improved, would raise the overall quality the most. More useful than general feedback.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Give me the version of this I'd be proud to show someone in 6 months."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Work that holds up over time is almost always clearer, more honest, and less reactive.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What does the data or evidence say, and where is it thin?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Separates assertion from evidence. If you can't point to evidence, you should know that before someone else points it out.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Rank these ideas by impact and explain your reasoning."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Forces prioritization with justification. The ranking matters less than the reasoning; that's where the real insight lives.</span></div>
  </div>
</div>

# 7. Sharpen the Output

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Make it tighter, clearer, more useful.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Cut this by 30% without losing any of the meaning."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Most first drafts are 30% longer than they need to be. This prompt does the edit you've been avoiding.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the one sentence that captures the entire idea?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">If you can't say it in one sentence, you probably don't have a clear enough point yet. This forces the distillation.</span></div>

Useful before sending anything to someone who hasn't been living inside the problem with you.</span></div>
  </div>

    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the clearest, most direct way to say this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Cuts through hedging, passive voice, and over-qualification. Good for anything that needs to land quickly or be acted on immediately.</span></div>

# 8. Simplify It

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it yet.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Explain this like I've never heard of it before."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The classic clarity test. Forces every assumption to be made explicit and every term to be defined.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the simplest version of this idea?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Strips away everything that isn't essential. Useful when something has grown complex through iteration and needs to be rebuilt from its core.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the jargon in here that an outsider wouldn't understand?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Flags the language that insiders use without realizing it's insider language. Especially useful for anything going to customers or execs.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Give me the two-sentence version."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">A forcing function for brevity. If it takes more than two sentences, you're probably still working through the idea rather than explaining it.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-purple text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the core idea underneath all of this complexity?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Sometimes complexity is a sign that the real idea hasn't been found yet. This asks Claude to dig past the layers to what's actually being said.</span></div>

# 9. Audience Lens

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">See it through someone else's eyes.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"How does this land for someone who is skeptical of the whole premise?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Useful before any high-stakes pitch or communication. If it doesn't hold up for the skeptic, it's not ready.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would the most senior person in the room think when they read this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Execs read differently than practitioners: faster, more focused on implications. This prompt simulates that read.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">

Useful for anything that crosses the technical/business divide.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>

Lets you address the objection before it's raised.</span></div>

    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-navy text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would make this feel relevant to someone who doesn't think this is their problem?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The hardest audience to write for. # 10. Force a Decision

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Stop analyzing, start deciding.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Given everything we've discussed, what's your recommendation?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Turns a conversation into a conclusion. Useful when you've explored enough and need Claude to synthesize, not just add more.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"If you had to choose one of these options right now, which would it be and why?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Eliminates the "it depends" answer. Forces a real stance and exposes the actual reasoning behind the recommendation.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the decision I'm avoiding, and what would you do?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Directly names the avoidance. Sometimes the most useful thing Claude can do is name the thing you've been circling around.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would you need to know to feel confident making a call here?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Identifies the missing information that's causing the stall. Useful when you're stuck in analysis and can't figure out why.</span></div>
  </div>

    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-royal text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Rank these options from best to worst and defend your top choice."</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The full commitment prompt. No hedging allowed. The defense is usually more valuable than the ranking.</span></div>

# 11. Test for Action

<div class="text-[13px] text-hg-medium italic mb-5">Make sure it leads somewhere.</div>
<div class="flex flex-col gap-3">
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">1</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What are the next three concrete steps someone should take after reading this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">The ultimate clarity test. If Claude can't name three concrete next steps, the output probably isn't specific enough to act on yet.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">2</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"Who needs to do what, by when, for this to move forward?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Converts insight into accountability. Useful at the end of any planning session, meeting summary, or strategy doc.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">3</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What would stop someone from acting on this, and how do you remove that obstacle?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Anticipates the friction before it happens. Most good ideas die not because they were wrong but because the path to action wasn't clear enough.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">4</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What does done look like? How do we know this worked?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Defines success criteria before you start. If you don't know what done looks like, you'll know when you're finished but not whether you succeeded.</span></div>
  </div>
  <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
    <div class="flex-shrink-0 px-2 py-1 rounded bg-hg-medium text-white text-[11px] font-bold w-6 text-center">5</div>
    <div><span class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">"What's the one thing someone should do today based on this?"</span><span class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark ml-2">Reduces everything to the single most important action. Good for ending any conversation where the output risks sitting in a doc and never moving.</span></div>
  </div>
</div>
