# 01 - Budget-Justified Language by Persona

<div class="flex gap-8 mt-6">
  <div class="flex-shrink-0 flex flex-col items-center gap-3 w-20">
    <div class="w-16 h-16 rounded-full bg-hg-navy text-white flex items-center justify-center text-[28px] font-bold">01</div>
    <HgIcon name="voice-customer" size="48px" />
  </div>
  <div class="flex-1 grid grid-cols-3 gap-5">
    <div class="hg-card">
      <div class="hg-card-header">What It Captures</div>
      <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">The exact words and phrases prospects use that signal a problem is worth spending budget on. Not emotional intensity - actual budget-justified framing: cost quantification, exec visibility, named line items, risk-based statements, and comparisons to existing spend. Broken out by IC, Director, and VP.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="hg-card">
      <div class="hg-card-header">Why It's Included</div>
      <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">The same problem sounds different depending on who's speaking. An IC describes pain operationally. A Director frames it as team risk. A VP frames it as org cost. Collapsing these into one voice produces messaging that resonates with none of them.</div>
    </div>
    <div class="hg-card">
      <div class="hg-card-header">What It Informs</div>
      <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">The campaign brief's core messaging matrix. The exact copy for emails, ads, and landing pages. Persona-specific subject lines. Battle card language for each buyer type. If messaging is built on customer words rather than internal positioning, conversion rates follow.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
