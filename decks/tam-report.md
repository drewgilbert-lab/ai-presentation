# The Method Every Analyst Firm Shares

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-8 mt-6">
  <div>
    <div class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[13px] uppercase tracking-wide mb-3">Two approaches. Same ceiling.</div>
    <div class="flex flex-col gap-4">
      <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
        <div class="flex-shrink-0 w-8 h-8 rounded-full bg-hg-navy text-white flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-[13px]">1</div>
        <div>
          <div class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">Survey-Based (Gartner, IDC, Forrester)</div>
          <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark mt-1">Poll buyers, aggregate responses, model market size. IDC surveys 300,000+ technology buyers annually. The output: <em>"Vendor A holds 23% of the CRM market."</em> Aggregate by design.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
      <div class="flex items-start gap-4">
        <div class="flex-shrink-0 w-8 h-8 rounded-full bg-hg-navy text-white flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-[13px]">2</div>
        <div>
          <div class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[14px]">Firmographic Segmentation (D&B, ZoomInfo)</div>
          <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark mt-1">Filter companies by industry, revenue, headcount, and estimate who might buy. D&B covers 600M+ business entities. Still produces estimates, not inventories.</div>
        </div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="mt-5 border-l-4 border-hg-red bg-hg-gray pl-4 py-3 rounded-r-md">
      <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark">Gartner describes its own TAM data as <span class="font-bold text-hg-navy">"a top-down starting point"</span> in its guidance documentation. Their words, not ours.</div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div>
    <div class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[13px] uppercase tracking-wide mb-3">What neither method produces</div>
    <div class="space-y-3">
      <div class="flex items-center gap-3 p-3 rounded-md bg-hg-gray">
        <div class="w-2 h-2 rounded-full bg-hg-red flex-shrink-0"></div>
        <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark">Which 4,000 companies in your territory are running a competitor's software right now</div>
      </div>
      <div class="flex items-center gap-3 p-3 rounded-md bg-hg-gray">
        <div class="w-2 h-2 rounded-full bg-hg-red flex-shrink-0"></div>
        <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark">Which accounts match your ICP but have zero solution in the category installed</div>
      </div>
      <div class="flex items-center gap-3 p-3 rounded-md bg-hg-gray">
        <div class="w-2 h-2 rounded-full bg-hg-red flex-shrink-0"></div>
        <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark">Which companies are actively researching this category based on real spend signals</div>
      </div>
      <div class="flex items-center gap-3 p-3 rounded-md bg-hg-gray">
        <div class="w-2 h-2 rounded-full bg-hg-red flex-shrink-0"></div>
        <div class="text-[13px] text-hg-dark">Actual company names, contacts, and sequenced outreach-ready account lists</div>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="mt-4 text-[13px] text-hg-dark font-bold text-hg-navy">These are execution questions. No analyst report answers them.</div>
  </div>
</div>

# The Gap Lives Between the Plan and the Pipeline

<div class="mt-4 mb-6 border-l-4 border-hg-royal bg-hg-light pl-6 py-4 rounded-r-md">
  <div class="font-bold text-hg-navy text-[15px] mb-1">The real failure mode</div>
  <div class="text-[14px] text-hg-dark">A go-to-market team launches a new segment, sized at $2.4B by a reputable analyst. By Q1, pipeline is half of model. Reps report that accounts they expected to be greenfield already have competing products installed. The territory was designed on estimated market composition — not actual technology deployment. No vendor switch fixes this. The method has to change.</div>
</div>

<div class="grid grid-cols-3 gap-5 mt-4">
  <div class="hg-card">
    <div class="hg-card-header">What Looked Right</div>
    <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">$2.4B analyst TAM. Credible source. Board-approved. Headcount hired. Segment launched.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="hg-card">
    <div class="hg-card-header">What Went Wrong</div>
    <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">Territory built on estimated market composition. Reps hit accounts already owned by competitors. Pipeline collapsed.</div>
  </div>
  <div class="hg-card">
    <div class="hg-card-header">What Was Missing</div>
    <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">Actual technology deployment data. Which companies match ICP, are greenfield, and are in-budget right now.</div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="mt-5 text-[13px] text-hg-dark text-center font-bold">The analyst report wasn't wrong. It answered a strategy question. You needed an execution answer.</div>

# Four Personas. One Gap. Different Pain.

<div class="grid grid-cols-2 gap-5 mt-5">
  <div class="hg-card">
    <div class="hg-card-header">VP Strategy / Corp Dev</div>
    <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">
      <p class="mb-2">Defended a $4.2B opportunity to the board. Headcount approved. Six months later, CRO asks why pipeline is half the model. <span class="font-bold text-hg-navy">The TAM didn't lie. It answered a different question than sales was trying to execute against.</span></p>
      <div class="border-l-2 border-hg-royal pl-3 mt-3 italic text-hg-royal">"You sized the market correctly. You just haven't sized <em>your</em> market yet."</div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="hg-card">
    <div class="hg-card-header">VP Sales / CRO</div>
    <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">
      <p class="mb-2">Reps handed quotas built on a market that exists in aggregate but not at the account level. Calling into companies that already have a competing solution installed. <span class="font-bold text-hg-navy">The number was never a list.</span></p>
      <div class="border-l-2 border-hg-royal pl-3 mt-3 italic text-hg-royal">"A TAM is a forecast. A territory is a list. No analyst report converts into one."</div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="hg-card">
    <div class="hg-card-header">CMO / Head of Demand Gen</div>
    <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">
      <p class="mb-2">Budget allocated to a segment based on market size data. Campaigns ran. MQLs came in. Pipeline quality was low because the segment was defined by category fit, not purchase readiness. <span class="font-bold text-hg-navy">Market size tells you where to fish. It doesn't tell you where the fish are biting.</span></p>
      <div class="border-l-2 border-hg-royal pl-3 mt-3 italic text-hg-royal">"You can be in the right lake and still come back empty."</div>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="hg-card">
    <div class="hg-card-header">RevOps / Sales Ops</div>
    <div class="hg-card-body text-[13px]">
      <p class="mb-2">Built the capacity plan, territory carve, and quota model off a top-down TAM. When actuals diverge from model, they get asked to explain the gap — and can't, because the inputs were never designed to produce account-level predictions. <span class="font-bold text-hg-navy">The model isn't broken. The inputs were the wrong resolution.</span></p>
      <div class="border-l-2 border-hg-royal pl-3 mt-3 italic text-hg-royal">"Analyst data is market-level resolution. Quota models need account-level resolution."</div>
